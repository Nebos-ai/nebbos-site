How Pearl works

Sharp where it counts. Quiet everywhere else.

Arrives prepared

Researched before day one

Each Pearl launches having studied best practice for its department and your industry, so it's useful from the first signal — not after months of training.

Asks rarely

One sharp question

When it genuinely can't explain something, Pearl asks the one person who can — about the specific data point, never about how anyone feels.

Shows its work

Confidence you can see

Every prediction carries a confidence score and a "why" you can open. Pearl would rather say "I'm not sure yet" than guess.

Human in the loop

Autonomy is earned, bounded, and reversible.

Pearl starts by suggesting. As its predictions prove out, you can let it act on the smaller things on its own — within limits you set. Before it acts on anything that matters, it can rehearse the move against a private copy of your data; anything consequential then pauses for a person, and any action it takes can be undone.
