About

We help organisations see what's about to happen.

Most software is a record of the past. We think the more useful thing is a clear, honest read of what's coming — early enough to do something about it, and explained well enough that a person can decide what.

The name

Why "Nebbos".

Nebbos is a small village in Vojvodina — the birthplace of Mihajlo Pupin, who spent his life on a single problem: how to carry a faint signal across great distance without losing what it held. His work let a weak voice travel further and arrive intact.

That's the idea we build on. Every organisation is full of faint operational signal that fades before anyone acts on it. Nebbos carries that signal further — and makes sure the meaning survives the trip.

What we believe

How we build.

Principle 01

Keep the human in the loop

Software should sharpen judgement, not replace it. The consequential calls stay with people, always.

Principle 02

Show your work

A prediction without its reasoning is just a guess with confidence. Every answer comes with its evidence.

Principle 03

Earn trust slowly

Autonomy is something a system earns by being right over time — not a switch you flip on day one.

What we build

Operations Intelligence, built on the Operational Graph.

Nebbos reads the work your organisation already does, builds a living map of how it operates, and answers the five questions that run underneath every operation: what's happening, what's about to go wrong, why, what to do, and what you've learned.

It's designed for the rules that govern AI that observes people — accountable by architecture, not by promise.
