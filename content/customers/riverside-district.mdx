Riverside coordinates across dozens of schools, hundreds of staff, and a stack of
compliance deadlines that cannot slip. The hard problems were rarely the big ones
— they were the handoffs nobody owned and the review dates that moved when a case
did.

## What changed

With a Pearl on operations and another on compliance, the district started seeing
the coverage gap and the slipping deadline **before** they reached a family or an
auditor — surfaced to the lead who could act, never broadcast to peers.

## The result

- Coverage gaps surfaced earlier, while a substitute could still be found.
- Compliance review dates tracked against the work actually happening.
- One live read across schools, instead of spreadsheets reconciled the night
  before a board meeting.

Every decision stayed with a person, and every action was recorded.
