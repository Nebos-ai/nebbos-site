Northbridge coordinates across a network of schools, the staff who run them, and
a calendar of compliance obligations that doesn't bend. The coordinator's hardest
mornings weren't the dramatic ones — they were the quiet gaps: the classroom
without a confirmed substitute, the mandated review whose date had quietly moved
when a case did.

## What changed

With a Pearl on operations and another on compliance, Northbridge began seeing the
problem before it became one. A substitute-coverage gap surfaced while a substitute
could still be found. An IEP review or a mandated check sliding toward its deadline
surfaced before the date arrived — to the **lead** who could act, never broadcast
across the network.

Crucially, Nebbos reads only the *shape* of this work — the patterns, the
thresholds, the deadlines. It does not read per-student records. FERPA-protected
detail stays where it belongs; what Nebbos watches is structural and aggregate:
*a review is due*, *a slot is uncovered*, never *who* or *why* at the level of an
individual student.

## The result

- Coverage gaps surfaced early, while a substitute could still be arranged.
- Mandated and IEP review dates tracked against the work actually happening.
- One live read across schools, instead of a reconciliation the night before.

Every surfaced signal went to a person to decide. Every action was recorded, so
the audit trail wrote itself — without a single per-student record leaving its
system.
